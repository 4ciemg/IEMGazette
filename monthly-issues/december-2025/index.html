# Tangzu x Azla Noirblanc Review

05-06/12/2025


Tangzu x Azla Noirblanc
Measurement dump and impressions


Acessories:
Comes with the usual things you see at this price: a round zip case (with a very cool motif on the cover), a 2 core modular cable, and a selection of tips that include Tangzu's Noble tips in all four sizes, and a set of Azla Origin tips. The cable is without a doubt my favorite stock cable. It's soft, pliable, has no memory, and looks incredible with the IEMs. The selection of tips is also great, and they are different enough to fit almost everyone (Nobles are short, and the Origin are quite long).

Build and comfort: These are fantastic. Compact and lightweight metal shells with slight contouring allows them to fit snuggly inside of the ear. and they are not thick at all, being very similar to the Q1 Pro, Bunny, and Gate. The faceplate is also gorgeous, and the biggest reason why I bought these with the elegant flower motif surrounded by a nice metal perimeter that highlights the shape of the IEM. They isolate quite well, a tiny bit above average.


Sound:
Not great. In fact, not good. It aims for HarmanIE2019, but somehow emphasizes the worst aspects: the midbass scoop is very pronounced, the 5-8 kHz region is boosted even above the 2-5 kHz ear gain, and there is little treble extension. And yet somehow, it is not the worst stock tuning I have.
It's just very polarizing, and frankly not something I can tolerate for long.

Channel Matching:
Not meaningful but quite disappointing. I expected a bit more precision here but it's within the realm of normal deviations.

Distortion:
Measured at 104 dB as per usual, and again there isn't much to see. It is interesting to see the change from second to third harmonic and back to second.

at least it looks good on the bookshelf. thats the most important thing

I've been using them without swapping for any other IEM ever since I got them.

The dreaded BA pos with 1% 3rd harmonic...


Insertion Depth:
Deeper does help, but treble extension is still a problem. Nothing major, but it's worth pointing out.
To make these sound good, EQ is mandatory. Thankfully the changes aren't complicated, and 3 filters (one for midbass, one for lowering 5-8 kHz region, one for treble extension) are enough to make this go from bad to excellent

Conclusion:
I bought these for one reason (the faceplate and overall beauty of it) and they not only delivered on that end, but also on a lot of other stuff such as the very good accessories and surprising comfort. And yes, I bought these knowing full well how they sounded and measured.
In the end, this is probably the best purchase I've made this year purely based off of enjoyment. Sound quality out of the box was bad, but does it really matter when it can be fixed easily and everything else is so good?
